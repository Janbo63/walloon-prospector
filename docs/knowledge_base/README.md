# Knowledge Base (RAG Staging) 📚🗺️

This directory serves as the local repository for all historical geological data, maps, surveys, and folklore papers. 

The documents stored here will form the "Brain" of your RAG system, allowing you to ask queries about high-probability geological zones and historical mining activity.

---

## 📂 Recommended Folder Structure

*   `knowledge_base/geology_surveys/`: Geological maps, surveys from the Polish Geological Institute (PIG), soil maps, and academic papers on Izera Foothills mineralogy.
*   `knowledge_base/history_folklore/`: Scanned documents, books, and articles about medieval Walloon mining paths, historic mining in Mirsk/Świeradów-Zdrój, and local folklore.
*   `knowledge_base/maps/`: Historical maps (e.g., German Messtischblatt maps, old geological sketches, LiDAR hillshade exports showing old shafts).

---

## 🧠 NotebookLM Sync & RAG
NotebookLM is an excellent fit for organizing this research. You can easily upload PDFs and text documents from this directory into your NotebookLM notebook.

### How to use NotebookLM with this directory:
1.  **Create Notebook**: Create a notebook named **"Sudeten Geology & Prospecting"**.
2.  **Upload Sources**: Upload PDFs of regional surveys, historical Walloon research papers, and your own compiled dog walk logs.
3.  **Chat & Query**: Query the notebook to cross-reference folklore paths with modern geological formations:
    *   *Question:* "Which old Walloon mining signs are associated with quartz or pyrite in the Mirsk area?"
    *   *Question:* "Are there historical records of copper or cobalt mining in the Izera Gneiss near Świeradów-Zdrój?"
4.  **Extract Lore**: Query NotebookLM to quickly generate drafts for your shop's "Lore & History" section on Specimen Cards.
