# Rock Tumbling Diary 🌀🪨

Document the journey of your found specimens from rough field rocks to polished, shop-ready specimens. Because tumbling takes several weeks, logging grit stages, dates, and results is critical to improving your output quality.

---

## 📝 Tumbling Run Template

Copy this template for each new tumbling batch:

```markdown
## Tumbling Run: [RUN-001]
*   **Barrel Size / Tumbler Type**: [e.g., 3lb Lortone Rotary]
*   **Start Date**: YYYY-MM-DD
*   **End Date**: YYYY-MM-DD
*   **Stones Included**: 
    *   `SPEC-001` (Milky Quartz)
    *   `SPEC-003` (Banded Gneiss)
    *   [Other rough stones...]

---

### 🟢 Stage 1: Coarse Grit (60/90 Silicon Carbide)
*   **Duration**: [e.g., 7 days] (YYYY-MM-DD to YYYY-MM-DD)
*   **Fill Level**: [e.g., 2/3 full, added plastic pellets for cushioning]
*   **Inspection Notes**: 
    *   Most quartz edges rounded nicely.
    *   `SPEC-001` has a deep crack, might need another week in Stage 1 to grind down.

### 🟡 Stage 2: Medium/Fine Grit (120/220 Silicon Carbide)
*   **Duration**: [e.g., 7 days] (YYYY-MM-DD to YYYY-MM-DD)
*   **Inspection Notes**: 
    *   Surface is completely smooth. Pits and cracks are mostly gone.
    *   Washed barrel and stones thoroughly to prevent grit contamination.

### 🔵 Stage 3: Pre-Polish (500/600 Aluminum Oxide)
*   **Duration**: [e.g., 7 days] (YYYY-MM-DD to YYYY-MM-DD)
*   **Inspection Notes**: 
    *   Stones have a matte sheen now. Looking excellent.

### ⚪ Stage 4: Polish (Aluminum Oxide or Cerium Oxide)
*   **Duration**: [e.g., 7-10 days] (YYYY-MM-DD to YYYY-MM-DD)
*   **Burnishing (Optional)**: [e.g., 24 hours with ivory soap flakes to clean polish film]
*   **Final Inspection Notes**: 
    *   `SPEC-001` turned out beautifully with a glass-like finish.
    *   `SPEC-003` has some softer feldspar bands that undercut slightly, but still looks stunning.
```
